Design Your Next Chapter

A Guided Vision Workbook for Women 60+
By Patricia Lapena | Reclaimed Vitality

Welcome

There comes a time in life when we begin asking different questions.

Not just:

  • What do I need to get through the day?
  • What do I have to manage?
  • What should I settle for now?

But deeper questions like:

  • What do I want this next chapter of life to feel like?
  • How do I want to live?
  • What would feel nourishing, peaceful, beautiful, and deeply aligned now?

This workbook is here to help you pause and listen.

Not to the voice that says, “It’s too late.” Not to the voice that says, “Be realistic.” But to the quieter, wiser voice inside you that still knows there is more available.

This is not about building a fantasy life. It is about becoming honest about what you want, what matters now, and what kind of life you are ready to create.

You do not have to figure it all out today. You simply need to begin.

How to Use This Workbook

Move through these pages gently.

You can write your answers in a journal, print these pages, or simply reflect and answer them out loud.

There are no perfect answers. There is only truth.

Try not to answer from habit. Answer from desire. Answer from wisdom. Answer from the version of you who is ready to reclaim her vitality, her joy, and her next chapter.

Part 1: Your Next Chapter Vision

1. Home & Environment

Close your eyes for a moment and picture the place where you feel most at peace.

  • What kind of home feels right for me now?
  • What do I want around me: water, nature, gardens, sunshine, quiet, community?
  • How do I want my home to feel when I wake up in the morning?
  • What would make this space feel beautiful, comforting, and supportive?
Journal prompt: My ideal home and environment in this next chapter looks like:

2. Health, Energy & Vitality

This next chapter is not only about where you live. It is also about how you feel inside your body.

  • How do I want to feel physically each day?
  • What kind of energy do I want to wake up with?
  • What do I want my body to be able to do with ease?
  • What habits would support me in feeling stronger, lighter, calmer, and more alive?
Journal prompt: In my next chapter, I want my health and energy to feel like:

3. Travel, Experiences & Adventure

This season of life can hold beauty, freedom, and new experiences.

  • Where do I want to go?
  • What experiences do I still want to enjoy?
  • What kind of travel excites me now: restful, adventurous, meaningful, luxurious, simple?
  • How often would I love to get away or experience something new?
Journal prompt: The experiences and adventures I want in this season of life are:

4. Nourishment & Daily Living

A beautiful life is often built through simple daily rhythms.

  • What do I want nourishment to look like?
  • How do I want meals, cooking, groceries, and dining out to feel?
  • What would make daily life feel easier, more enjoyable, and less rushed?
  • What routines help me feel grounded and cared for?
Journal prompt: The daily rhythms and nourishment I want in my life are:

5. Freedom, Time & Space

Many women spend years caring for everyone else. This is your invitation to consider what freedom looks like for you.

  • How do I want my days to feel?
  • Where do I want more spaciousness?
  • What am I ready to release?
  • What would it feel like to have more time for myself, my health, my relationships, and what brings me joy?
Journal prompt: The kind of freedom and spaciousness I want in my next chapter is:

6. Beauty, Self-Care & Support

Support is not a luxury. It can be part of a well-lived life.

  • What would make life feel more supported and enjoyable?
  • What kind of self-care feels nourishing to me?
  • What would help me feel confident, cared for, and expressed?
  • Where do I need more help, softness, or ease?
Journal prompt: The kind of support and self-care I want in this next chapter is:

7. Relationships, Community & Contribution

Vitality is not only physical. It also lives in connection.

  • Who do I want to spend more time with?
  • What kind of community do I want around me?
  • How do I want to feel in my friendships and relationships?
  • Where do I want to give, encourage, contribute, or inspire?
Journal prompt: The relationships and community I want to create or deepen are:

8. Purpose, Passion & Possibility

There is still more in you.

  • What still wants to come through me?
  • What dream have I postponed?
  • What kind of work, service, creativity, or leadership still calls me?
  • If I stopped shrinking, what might I allow myself to build, share, or become?
Journal prompt: What still feels possible for me is:

Part 2: What Would Support This Life?

Once you have a clearer picture of the life you want, the next question becomes:

What would help support this life emotionally, physically, and financially?

You do not need to solve everything at once. This section is simply about clarity.

  • What kind of support would help me feel healthier and more energized?
  • What habits or structures would help me stay aligned?
  • What kind of community would help me keep going?
  • What resources would help me create more freedom?
  • What kind of income, if any, would help make this vision feel sustainable?
Journal prompt: To support the life I want, I may need:

Part 4: What This Means

  • You want more than you have been admitting
  • You are craving energy, health, and renewed purpose
  • You are ready for a more beautiful and supported way of living
  • You need a clearer path for your next chapter
  • You may also want to create additional income to support the life you desire

All of that is valid.

Awareness is not indulgence. It is information. And information can become transformation when you act on it with support.
